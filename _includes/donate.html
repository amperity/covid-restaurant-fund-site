The Plate Fund provides emergency financial assistance to hard-hit restaurant and food-service workers who have lost jobs and income because of the COVID-19 pandemic.

The crisis is especially devastating to the restaurant industry.

When COVID-19 hit the Seattle area, more than 100,000 people were employed at some 5,000 food establishments in King County. Most restaurants have been forced to close or reduce operations, and many have laid off the majority of their staffs. A vast majority of line cooks, wait staff, dishwashers and other employees rely on every paycheck, as well as tips, to support themselves and their families.

For many, these payments will “bridge the gap” between the time they received their last paychecks and when they can finally access federal stimulus payments and/or unemployment insurance benefits. For others, it may be the only assistance they receive.
