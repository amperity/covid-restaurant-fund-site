Frequently Asked Questions

Why are restaurant workers affected differently than employees in other industries?

The COVID-19 pandemic has been especially challenging for the tens of thousands of people employed by more than 5,000 restaurants in King County. Most businesses have been forced to cut hours, lay off workers, and close temporarily or permanently. This is especially true for single-location and small businesses, which do not have the resources to pay severance or otherwise support people they must let go.

Many restaurant workers also rely on every paycheck, and have urgent needs that must be paid - food, medicine, housing - before the federal stimulus payments and/or unemployment insurance benefits kick in.

A higher proportion of restaurant workers also are undocumented and not eligible for federal stimulus payments or unemployment benefits. And, unemployment benefits will likely be lower for restaurant workers than many others who have been laid off because many of them work part-time. What’s more, many restaurant workers also are not familiar with how to access assistance, because they’ve never had to seek out assistance before.

How is The Plate Fund different from other efforts helping people financially impacted by the pandemic?

The fund is different from most other services currently available in King County because it provides immediate and direct financial assistance to individuals, not to organizations.

How will the fund be distributed?

Monies from the fund will be transferred directly to individuals via check or electronic means so they may immediately pay for what they need.

What organizations are behind the effort?

The Schultz Family Foundation created the fund, which has been seeded with $4 million in partnership with All In Seattle, the Seattle Foundation and UpTogether, as well as with restaurant owners and other local organizations, including: Charlie’s Produce; Amperity; Seattle Restaurant Alliance; Seattle Restaurants United; Washington Hospitality Association; Starbucks Foundation; Tom Douglas and Eric Tanaka (Tom Douglas Seattle Kitchen); Renee Erickson (Bateau, The Whale Wins, The Walrus and the Carpenter, among others); Efrem Fesaha (Boon Boona Coffee); Julian Hagood (Harry’s Fine Foods, Harry’s Beach House); Wassef Haroun (Mamnoon); Rodney Hines (Metier Brewing Company); Taylor Hoang (Taylor Hoang Restaurants Inc., Seatac Airport Restaurants, Pho Cyclo Cafe, District One Saigon); Trey Lamont (Jerk Shack); Edouardo Jordan (JuneBaby, Salare, Lucinda); Melissa Miranda (Musang); Donna Moodie (Marjorie’s); Tamara Murphy and Linda Di Lello Morton (Terra Plata); and Ethan Stowell (Ethan Stowell Restaurants).
