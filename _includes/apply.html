Many of your lives have been upended as your employers have been forced to cut hours, suspend their business, permanently close and lay off employees.

If you have urgent, necessary expenses like groceries, utilities, rent, medicine or baby food that you are unable to pay for until you receive federal assistance or unemployment insurance, or if you do not qualify for those benefits, The Plate Fund may be able to assist during this difficult time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to receive funds?

Anyone who lives in King County, worked in a restaurant or food service establishment and has experienced a reduction in hours or been laid off as a result of the COVID-19 crisis may apply, including dishwashers, cooks, servers, bartenders, food truck employees and cafe attendants.

What documentation will applicants need to apply?

Applicants will be required to provide documentation to verify that they live in King County, worked in a restaurant and were laid off as a result of the COVID-19 crisis. Applicants will be required to:

  • Upload a pay stub dated on or after March 1, 2020;
  • Upload one form of government issued identification or two forms of alternate identification that includes an applicantâ€™s residential address;
  • Demonstrate that their gross income is no more than $62,000 per year, inclusive of tips;
  • Verify that they have experienced a decrease in work hours or loss of their job.
Who is reviewing the applications? How are they analyzed?

UpTogether, a digital capital exchange platform that helps low-income people accelerate their economic mobility, will review and process applications. Payments will be disbursed based on the order in which they are received so long as applicants are eligible and submit all of the required documentation.
